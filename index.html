About this Switchboard Heat Load Calculator

This free online calculator from SparkyCalc implements the IEC TR 60890 method for verifying temperature rise in low-voltage switchgear and controlgear assemblies. It is designed for electrical engineers, switchboard manufacturers, panel builders and maintenance engineers who need to size cooling fans, anti-condensation heaters, heat exchangers, or air conditioning units for electrical enclosures.

The calculator supports all seven IEC TR 60890 installation configurations, a built-in component loss library with over 50 common devices (MCBs, MCCBs, ACBs, contactors, VFDs, soft starters, CTs, busbars and more), PDF report export for documentation, and save/load configurations so you can return to previous calculations.

Cooling Fan Sizing

When the internal heat gain of a switchboard (QG) exceeds the heat the enclosure can dissipate through its walls (QS), forced ventilation is required. The calculator determines the required airflow rate V in m3/h, litres per second, and CFM. It applies the altitude correction factor f, fan efficiency, and any inlet temperature rise to give you an accurate fan specification for your site elevation.

Anti-Condensation Heater Sizing

In cold or humid environments, a switchboard heater prevents moisture condensation on busbars and insulation. The IEC TR 60890 heater formula Q = A x k x (Ti - Tu) gives the minimum heater wattage needed to maintain the internal temperature above the dew point. The calculator rounds up to the nearest standard heater size and provides installation guidance.

Natural Convection Check

Many enclosures can reject sufficient heat through their surfaces without any active cooling. This mode calculates the surface heat dissipation QS and compares it to the internal heat gain QG. If QS exceeds QG, no fan is required. A surface finish correction factor c2 accounts for enclosure colour and material finish.

Heat Exchangers & Air Conditioning

For sealed enclosures with high IP ratings where open ventilation is not acceptable, the calculator supports heat exchanger sizing (using effectiveness factor) and air conditioning capacity sizing (using the Coefficient of Performance, COP). Safety factors can be applied to all cooling methods.

How IEC TR 60890 Works

The IEC TR 60890 method calculates heat flow in three stages. First, the effective surface area A of the enclosure is determined using one of seven installation-specific formulas that account for which faces are exposed to free air. Second, the surface heat dissipation QS = k x A x dT is calculated, where k is the heat transfer coefficient of the enclosure material and dT is the temperature difference between internal and ambient air. Third, the residual heat QR = QG - QS determines whether active cooling is needed.

If QR is positive, forced ventilation or another active cooling method is required. The required airflow for a fan is V = f x QR / dT, where f is an altitude-dependent air constant ranging from 3.1 at sea level to 5.2 at 3,500 m altitude.

Supported Installation Configurations

IEC TR 60890 defines seven standard installation geometries, each with a different effective surface area formula. These include single free-standing switchboards (all faces exposed), wall-mounted switchboards (rear face against wall), end-of-suite panels (one side shared), mid-suite panels (both sides shared), and wall-mounted with roof covering (maximum shielding). Selecting the correct installation type is critical for accurate results.

Frequently Asked Questions

What standard does this calculator follow?
This calculator implements the IEC TR 60890 method for temperature-rise verification of low-voltage switchgear and controlgear assemblies by calculation. The formulas are consistent with both the 2014 (second edition) and 2022 (third edition) versions of the standard.
Can I use this for motor control centres (MCCs)?
Yes. IEC TR 60890 applies to all low-voltage switchgear and controlgear assemblies, including motor control centres, main switchboards, distribution boards, power distribution units, and PLC control panels, provided they are enclosed assemblies.
What is the heat transfer coefficient k?
The heat transfer coefficient k describes how well the enclosure material conducts and radiates heat. Per IEC TR 60890, sheet steel and aluminium use k = 5.5 W/m2K, stainless steel uses k = 5.1 W/m2K, and plastic or polyester enclosures use k = 3.5 W/m2K. High IP-rated enclosures should be derated accordingly.
Why does altitude affect fan sizing?
At higher altitudes, air is less dense and has a lower heat capacity per unit volume. The same airflow rate carries less heat at altitude than at sea level. The IEC TR 60890 air constant f corrects for this, meaning a larger fan is needed at high altitude to achieve the same cooling effect.
What safety margin should I apply to fan sizing?
IEC TR 60890 does not mandate a specific safety factor, but industry practice is to select a fan rated at 20% above the calculated airflow to account for filter mat fouling over time. This calculator allows you to apply safety factors from 1.0 to 1.5.
Does this calculator comply with IEC 61439?
IEC TR 60890 provides the calculation method referenced by IEC 61439-1 for temperature rise verification by calculation. This calculator implements that method and can be used as part of an IEC 61439 design verification package when combined with appropriate engineering review.
